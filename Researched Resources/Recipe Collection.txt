1)White Rice (Servings:6 ; Cooktime:25 minutes)

Ingredients:

-Water 4 - 1/2 cup;
-White rice - 3 cup;
-Salt - 1tsp;

Directions:

-Step 1: Bring water with salt to a boil in a 4-quart heavy saucepan with a tight-fitting lid.
-Step 2: Add rice and stir once, then reduce heat to low and cook, covered, 20 minutes.
-Step 3: Remove pan from heat (do not lift lid) and let stand, covered, 5 minutes.
-Step 4 Fluff rice gently with a fork and serve.

-Step 9: Put the tomatoes on top of the chicken 
-Step 10: Finish by covering with the remaining half of the bagel and serve.

-Step 3: Pour off fat from skillet

1)Spicy cucumber pickle

Ingredients:
6 pickling cucumbers , or 2 regular-sized cucumbers
2 banana shallots
2 teaspoons mustard seeds
½ teaspoon ground turmeric
2 star anise
75 g caster sugar
150 ml vinegar

Steps:
1 - Cut the pickling cucumbers in half lengthways
2 - Slice regular ones through the middle, then into fingers
3 - Peel and finely slice the shallots.
4 - Put the cucumbers and shallots in a colander, Sprinkle over 2 teaspoons of sea salt and let it settle.
5 - After 45 minutes of settling, rinse well.
6 - Combine all the other ingredients in a pan and bring to the boil. Stir until the sugar dissolves.
7 - Fit the cucumbers snugly into a Kilner jar, then pour over the liquid. 
8 - Seal and let it settle for 24 hours, then you can enjoy it

2)Chickpea curry

Ingredients:
2 medium onions
1 x 400 g tin chickpeas
1-2 fresh green chillies
2 tablespoons sunflower oil
1 teaspoon mustard seeds
½ teaspoon asafoetida
1-2 teaspoon sugar , optional
½ x 400 g tin of tomatoes
1 teaspoon fresh ginger paste
1 teaspoon garlic paste
1 teaspoon green chilli paste
1 teaspoon red chilli powder
1 teaspoon turmeric
1 teaspoon dhana jeera powder
1-2 teaspoon sugar , optional
a few sprigs of fresh coriander
½ teaspoon garam masala

Steps:
1 - Peel and finely chop the onions
2 - drain and rinse the tin chickpea
3 - finely slice the green chillies. 
4 - Heat the oil in a saucepan over a medium heat and add the mustard seeds
5 - Once the seeds are crackling, add the asafoetida.
6 - Then stir in the onions and fry until transparent and turning golden at the edges.
7 - Chop and add the tinned tomatoes
8 - Cook for another 5 minutes
9 - Add your spices: ginger, garlic, green chilli pastes, red chilli powder, dhana jeera and a pinch of sea salt.
10 - Add sugar if you would like to
11 - Keep stirring and cook for another 5 minutes.
12 - Stir the chickpeas into the pan.
13 - Add 250ml water to make a thick sauce. Place the lid on the pan, lower the heat and simmer for 5 to 7 minutes.
14 - Pick and finely chop the coriander
15 - Then sprinkle onto the saucepan the coriander along with the garam masala and sliced green chillies, to serve.

3) Gluten Free Chocolate brownies

Ingredients:
75 ml sunflower oil , plus extra for greasing
6 tbsp ground flaxseed
1 x 400g tin of aduki beans
200 g soft light brown sugar
55 g cocoa powder
55 g ground almonds
1 tsp gluten-free baking powder
1 tsp vanilla extract
100 g dark dairy-free chocolate
200 ml coconut cream
3 tbsp icing sugar
1/2 tsp vanilla extract

Steps:
1 - Place the packet of coconut cream in the fridge and leave for 3-4 hours to thicken up.
2 - Preheat the oven to 180°C/350°F/gas 4.
3 - Lightly grease a 23cm square brownie tin with sunflower oil and line with baking paper.
4 - In a bowl, combine the ground flaxseed with 9 tablespoons of water, stir well and leave to one side to thicken up.
5 - Drain and rinse the aduki beans, then put in a food processor and blitz to a smooth paste.
6 - Add the flaxseed mixture, sunflower oil, sugar, cocoa, ground almonds, baking powder and vanilla extract into the food processor
7 - Then pulse everything again until you have a rich and glossy batter.
8 - Roughly chop the dark chocolate into small chunks, add to the mixture and pulse briefly just to incorporate the chocolate.
9 - Spoon the batter into the prepared brownie tin and bake for 60 minutes, keep an eye incase you notice any burn then remove it earlier.
10 - Set aside tin to cool.
11 - In a bowl, whisk the chilled coconut cream, icing sugar and vanilla extract until thick and creamy.
12 - Slice the brownie into squares and serve with the coconut whip for drizzling.

1)Leek and mushroom pasta
    
200g chestnut mushrooms
Olive oil
3 garlic cloves
2 fresh rosemary sprigs
3 leeks
125ml dry white wine
90ml soya cream
350g pasta
parsley


1 - Heat a good knob of butter in a pan. 
2 - Add garlic and rosemary and let it settle for 30 seconds.
3 - Add the mushrooms and fry over a high heat for 5 minutes until they’re golden brown.
4 - Season the mushrooms with preferred spices, then spoon onto a plate and set aside.
5 - Add another knob of butter to the pan
6 - Fry the leeks over a low heat for 10 minutes until softened.
7 - Add the wine and bubble until mostly evaporated
8 - Then add the soya cream, season well.
9 - Then mix in the garlicky mushrooms.
10 - Meanwhile, add the pasta to boiling water for 10 minutes
11 - Drain the water and mix with the leeks and mushrooms.
12 - Season and add in a handful of chopped fresh parsley, then serve.

3)Vegan / lactose free French Cassoulet

3 cups canned white beans, drained and washed
8 oz package of tempeh, cubed into ½-inch pieces
1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
1 medium onion, diced
2 medium carrots, sliced
2 stalks of celery, diced
5 cloves garlic, finely minced
15 mushrooms sliced
2 tomatoes diced
1 tbsp chopped sage
1 tbsp chopped thyme
2 bay leaves
2 tbsp parsley, chopped
½ cup red wine, optional
Salt and ground black pepper to taste

1 - Heat oil in a large saucepan.
2 - Add the tempeh cubes and saute stir frequently until the tempeh is golden-brown.
3 - Add the onions, garlic, celery and carrots. 
4 - Add a pinch of salt and some ground black pepper
5 - Saute the vegetables for five minutes until the onions are translucent.
6 - Add the mushrooms and the wine. 
7 - Cook until the moisture from the vegetables and all of the water has evaporated.
8 - Add the tomatoes and beans along with the bay, sage, parsley and thyme leaves.
9 - Bring to a boil -- add water if the stew is too thick - and turn the heat to a level where it boils gently.
10 - Add salt and pepper. 
11 - Serve hot with some left over bread.

1)Veg Fajita Roll
Ingredients
-400g can black beans, drained
-small bunch coriander, finely chopped
-4 large or 8-12 small flour tortillas
-1 avocado, sliced, or 1 small tub guacamole
-2 tbsp soured cream

Steps
Step 1. To make the fajita mix, take two or three strips from each colour of pepper and finely chop them. Set aside.
Step 2. Heat the oil in a frying pan
Step 3. Fry the remaining pepper strips and the onion until soft and starting to brown at the edges.
Step 4. Cool slightly and mix in the chopped raw peppers.
Step 5. Add the garlic and cook for 1 min, then add the spices and stir.
Step 6. Cook for a couple of mins more until the spices become aromatic
Step 7. Then add half the lime juice and season. Transfer to a dish, leaving any juices behind, and keep warm.
Step 8. Tip the black beans into the same pan, then add the remaining lime juice and plenty of seasoning
Step 9. Stir the beans around the pan to warm them through and help them absorb any flavours then stir through the coriander.
Step 10. Warm the tortillas in a microwave or in a low oven, then wrap them so they don’t dry out.
Step 11. Serve the tortillas with the fajita mix, beans, avocado and soured cream for everyone to help themselves.

2)Spicy Mexican Rice
Ingredients
-groundnut oil 1 tbsp
-onion 1 large, finely chopped
-garlic 2 cloves, crushed
-green chilli 1, sliced
-green pepper 1, diced
-basmati rice 200g
-chipotle paste 1 tbsp
-chopped tomatoes 400g tin
-vegetable stock 250ml
-coriander a handful, chopped
-lime wedges to serve

Steps
Step 1. Heat the oil in a deep lidded frying pan.
Step 2. Cook the onion, garlic, chilli and pepper for 10 minutes until starting to soften.
Step 3. Stir in the basmati, then cook for 3-4 minutes, stirring all the time.
Step 4. Mix in the chipotle paste and cook, stirring for a couple of minutes until fragrant
Step 5. Then tip in the tomatoes and stock, and stir well.
Step 6. Bring to a simmer turning the heat down to low 
Step 7. Put on a lid and cook for 15 minutes or until the rice is cooked through.
Step 8. Stir through the coriander and serve with lime wedges for squeezing.

3)Pasta salad with bocconcini, capers and tomatoes
Ingredients
-short pasta (such as radiatori) 150g
-vine tomatoes 3, diced
-olive oil 2 tbsp
-red wine vinegar 1 tbsp
-baby capers 1 tbsp
-bocconcini 150g pack, drained and balls torn in half if large (see notes below)
-basil a handful, chopped
-chives chopped to make 2 tbsp
